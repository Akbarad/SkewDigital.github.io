Is your website better than your competitor’s

8 parameters to evaluate your website

Scroll Down
Website
  • 232 Views
  • 17th March 2020

Wondering why your website doesn’t attract enough customers while your competitor seems to have most of his business solely through their website. It seems you are doing everything right, but they have managed to have a more attractive, user-friendly, website and attract more customers away from you.

You may need a change - A paradigm shift to outperform your competitor's website. But how do you measure it? Have you recovered or can you still improve things?


To find out what matters when it comes to removing the noise and building a better website than your competitors, here are 8 things you should use to measure success at work. By overcoming these limitations, your website will become the best salesperson for your business and your competitors won't know what hit them!

Design and User Interface

Your website’s user interface is the make or break point of how memorable it can be. After all, everyone wants a website that looks slick, works like a wonder and doesn’t make their customers feel like they need a degree to navigate it. But how can you make sure your website looks better than your competition? Here’s how:


• Look at how your website fares when compared to theirs in terms of Textual content, Layout and Visuals. Do you have more Images/VIdeos? Is the layout more appealing? Does the design of your contact page have details that inspire trust yet not overbearing to the visitors?


• Focus on consistency. Consistent colours, fonts and elements will make your website look professional – something that inconsistent data will fail to impress.


• Make sure all of your pages load quickly. Nobody wants to wait for ages for a page to load—if yours takes forever to show up, you can bet your customers will be heading over to your competitors before you know it

Page Load Time

Let's face it: no one got time to wait. If your website isn't loading fast enough, you're likely to lose out on potential customers. A website's page load time should be a fraction of a second – that's how long you have to make an impression. Believe me, the only thing that can make that uncomfortable silence longer is if your competitor's website loads faster than yours!

To make sure your speed stays up to scratch, take a good look at the size of your images and videos. It is beneficial to compress them (not too much though!) and make sure they don't take more time than necessary to download. We recommend paying attention to any third party scripts being used too – if there are too many of them, consider cutting down so that you don't have any delays slowing down your visitors.

Let me tell ya – optimizing page load speed is tricky business! But it's essential if you want your website to be better than your competitors – no one wants to be bogged down in a slow-loading page while their competition is zooming ahead!

Think of SEO Optimization as putting an ad in the newspaper, or a radio spot – there's no point having a good product if no one knows about it. And Crawl-ability as making sure that search engines can recognize and understand the information contained in the pages of your website.

Responsive Across Devices

It's not enough to just launch a website—you want to make sure it looks and feels great on any device. That's where responsiveness comes in—apart from looking cool, it also helps with your search engine rankings. So when you're checking out your competitors' websites,see if they've got mobile-friendly versions, or if they've been properly optimized for desktop and tablet devices.



But there's still more to consider when it comes to responsiveness. For example:

•Is the site loading quickly on all devices? Large images or too many code-heavy elements can slow it down.


•Are links easy to click on regardless of device size? Nobody wants to struggle with fat fingers on tiny phones.


•Are the fonts easy to read at different sizes? Responsive typography is key so that people can understand what you're saying!

When responsive design is done properly, it means that user experience should be consistent no matter what type of device they use—and that means people will stick around longer. And as we all know, longer stays mean better customer retention rates—now that's something worth striving for!

Security & Privacy

The fifth point on your website better-than-competitor checklist is Security & Privacy. Having a secure and private website is so important—but there are way more ninja-level digital security moves you can take to make sure your site takes it up a notch.



For starters, make sure your web apps have up-to-date security patches and that they're running in an isolated environment where they can't impact the entire system if there's ever a security breach. And beyond the technical stuff, you should also be aware of regulatory risks and what data protection measures you need to abide by.


So don't just rely on your hosting provider to provide the necessary security protocols—ask yourself what else you can do to bump up the privacy settings and keep sensitive information under lock and key. A few ideas worth exploring include:

• Investing in advanced encryption systems

• Choosing secure payment options

• Implementing proper identity management & access control systems

• Regularly running tests with penetration tools

• Establishing strict rules for data storage & handling

•Being transparent about data collection and usage policies

Put together, these steps will give you a more secure website than your competitors, which means peace of mind for not only yourself but for your visitors too.

User Experience & Interaction

User experience (UX) and interaction are some of the most important elements when you're talking about a website. Think about it—how often have you gone to a website, only to give up within seconds because it was so clunky and complicated? UX and Interaction – they define how usable your website is, while presenting your audience with an easy to navigate platform.


To make sure your site beats the competition, here's what you should check off:


  • Responsiveness

    • s your website designed to be mobile-friendly? If not, then it's time for an upgrade! Having a responsive design means people can access your site anytime, anywhere—and that’s a feature worth its weight in gold.

  • Browser Compatibility

    • How many browsers are supported by your website? Have you tested it on Firefox, Chrome and Safari? If not, then you're missing out on potential customers who use alternative browsers.

  • Page Loading Speed

    • Does it take forever for each page to load? Research shows that even a one-second delay in page loading time can lead to 7% reduction in conversions. Yikes! Test those speeds and make sure they stay under the one second mark.

  • Competitor Analysis

    • We pay attention to what competitors are doing so we can tailor our approaches accordingly

  • Navigation

    • Are you making things difficult for yourself by having non-intuitive navigation menus? Aim for simple menus with clearly visible categories marks - no customer wants to get lost trying to find what they need.

Content Quality and Structure

If you think website content is merely of words and text, then you’re in for a surprise. Content also includes every word in an image, every video caption on your Youtube, and every infographic. And let’s not forget the structure of content—how it flows, its impact on usability, readability, and overall design


It's no surprise that content quality matters—it's the difference between your website being a boring relic and one that engages customers and drives sales. Here are some parameters to judge if your content is better than your competitors:

• Accurate & Relevant Content: Is the information published on your site up-to-date in terms of accuracy? Is it relevant to your target audience? An outdated & irrelevant content can affect user experience negatively as well as hurt search engine ranking.

• Quality & User Experience: Think about how users interact with your website content—from the formatting of headings and subheadings to ease of navigation within post pages.

• Speed & Consistency: How fast does your web page load? Do fonts look consistent across devices? Are images properly compressed to load faster? A delay of more than three seconds causes 53% of users to abandon focus from a web page, adversely affecting SEO rankings too!

• Search Engine Optimized: Does each webpage have appropriate keywords used in meta tags and meta descriptions? Are headings used efficiently with proper SEO optimization? SEO has a direct effect on the visibility of a webpage in search engines; therefore it’s important to be mindful while creating content for the website.
